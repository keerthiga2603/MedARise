Asthma Care Guide

Follow these guidelines for better asthma management, based on the severity of your condition.

For Beginning Stage Asthma

Do's:

  • Take medications as prescribed by your doctor regularly.
  • Monitor your symptoms daily and use a peak flow meter if recommended.
  • Know your asthma triggers and avoid them as much as possible.
  • Exercise regularly to keep your lungs healthy, but avoid strenuous activities in cold or polluted environments.
  • Keep your environment clean by using air purifiers and avoiding smoke.

Don'ts:

  • Do not skip your medication or change your medication without consulting your doctor.
  • Avoid exposure to dust, pollen, and smoke.
  • Don’t ignore early symptoms like coughing or wheezing; treat them immediately to prevent an attack.
  • Do not expose yourself to cold, dry air, as it may trigger symptoms.
  • Do not take excessive stress, as anxiety can worsen asthma symptoms.

For Moderate Stage Asthma

Do's:

  • Follow your prescribed treatment plan consistently.
  • Use a rescue inhaler (bronchodilator) when necessary, as per your doctor's advice.
  • Track your symptoms and peak flow readings regularly to monitor changes.
  • Ensure your home is allergen-free by keeping it well-ventilated and reducing allergens.
  • Attend regular follow-up appointments with your healthcare provider.

Don'ts:

  • Don’t ignore any changes in your breathing patterns or symptoms.
  • Do not skip your follow-up appointments with your healthcare provider.
  • Avoid exposure to known allergens like pet dander, mold, or dust mites.
  • Do not wait too long to use your rescue inhaler if symptoms worsen.
  • Don’t attempt to exercise in an environment that triggers your asthma symptoms.

For Severe Stage Asthma

Do's:

  • Take your medications as prescribed, including long-term control and rescue medications.
  • Carry your rescue inhaler with you at all times, and use it when needed.
  • Ensure that your home is free from environmental triggers, including cigarette smoke, mold, and dust.
  • Work closely with your healthcare provider to adjust treatment plans as necessary.
  • Consider having an asthma action plan in place to manage symptoms during emergencies.

Don'ts:

  • Don’t delay seeking medical help if your symptoms worsen or if you're struggling to breathe.
  • Avoid stressful situations that may provoke asthma attacks.
  • Do not expose yourself to strong smells, chemicals, or air pollutants.
  • Don’t reduce or stop your medication without consulting your doctor, even if you feel better.
  • Do not ignore emergency symptoms like rapid breathing, inability to speak, or chest tightness.
